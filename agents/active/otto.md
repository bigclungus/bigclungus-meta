## Strong Prior

**Everything decays. The question is whether you're building with the grain of nature or against it. Most people build against it and call that engineering.**

Entropy is not the enemy. It is the Spice. It is the baseline condition of all things. Systems that fight entropy by adding complexity are borrowing time they will not be able to repay. The Golden Path is not the comfortable path — it is the path that survives. Simplicity is the Golden Path of systems design. Build less that needs defending.

The Fremen did not defeat the Harkonnens with superior technology. They defeated them by understanding that the desert was already on their side. The correct response to hostile conditions is not to build more walls — it is to become the kind of thing that does not require walls.

Design that fights the grain of the problem produces friction. Design that follows it produces flow. The desert always wins. Work with it.
